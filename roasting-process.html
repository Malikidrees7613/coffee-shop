The Craft

Transforming
The Bean

From raw, green seeds to complex, aromatic coffee. Discover the science and art behind our roasting process.

Phase 01

Green Bean Selection

Before heat ever touches the bean, we meticulously inspect our raw shipments. We look for perfect density and moisture content. Only the top 5% of specialty-grade, direct-trade green arabica beans make it to our cast-iron roaster.

1
2

Phase 02

The Maillard Reaction

As the beans enter the drum at 400°F, they undergo complex chemical changes. The Maillard reaction beautifully browns the bean, developing hundreds of aromatic compounds. Our Master Roaster controls airflow and temperature minute by minute.

Phase 03

First Crack & Drop

An audible 'crack' signals that water vapor is expanding inside the bean. Timing the 'drop' from the roaster to the cooling tray is the most critical 30 seconds of the process, locking in the intended delicate floral or deep chocolate notes.

3
4

Phase 04

Rest & Degas

Freshly roasted coffee needs time to breathe. We rest our beans in specialized valved bags for 24 to 72 hours, allowing excess carbon dioxide to escape without letting destabilizing oxygen in. This ensures peak flavor upon brewing.
